Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 25, 2026

Wattlog ("the app") is a home energy tracking tool. This policy explains what information the app handles and how.

What we collect

Wattlog stores the energy data you log yourself — grid power on/off events, generator fuel and run hours, and prepaid meter purchases. This data is stored locally on your device using browser storage and is not transmitted to any server or third party.

Pi Network authentication and payments

If you choose to use the "Support Wattlog" feature, the app uses the Pi Network SDK to authenticate you and process a Pi payment. In this process:

  • Pi Network provides your app-specific user identifier to Wattlog, as permitted by the scopes you approve.
  • Payment details (amount, memo, payment ID) are sent to Wattlog's server functions solely to approve and complete the payment through Pi's official payment API.
  • Wattlog does not store your Pi wallet address, balance, or transaction history beyond what is needed to complete a single payment.

What we don't do

  • We do not sell or share your data with advertisers or third parties.
  • We do not track you across other apps or websites.
  • We do not require an account, email, or phone number to use the core energy-tracking features.

Data deletion

Since your energy log data lives in your browser's local storage, you can clear it at any time by clearing your browser's site data for this app, or by using your device's app storage settings if accessed through Pi Browser.

Changes to this policy

If this policy changes, the updated version will be posted at this same URL with a new "Last updated" date.

Contact

Questions about this policy can be sent to the developer contact listed on Wattlog's Pi Developer Portal listing.
